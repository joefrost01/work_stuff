We are reshaping the Markets data-products Confluence space
The current space has grown organically around the work, which makes content hard to find and hard to evidence. The new structure is organised around our data products instead. This email covers what changes, where things now live, and what you need to do differently.
Why we are doing this
Three reasons:
1.  Teams change; data products do not. Organising by product means a reorg never breaks the documentation.
2.  Audit, risk and security need consistent paths to evidence. Every product page now has an identical structure, so governance is always in the same place.
3.  Cross-cutting views (releases, reconciliations, ADRs, Jira work) will assemble themselves from labels instead of being maintained by hand.
 
Where things now live
Zone What you will find there
How We Work Architecture, the component catalogue, ADR log, paved-road infrastructure docs, engagement process, standards and templates. The label taxonomy lives here.
Data Products One page per ODP, FDP and CDP, each with the same eight children: overview, data model, governance, lineage, DQ & reconciliations, operations, decisions, work in flight. The Product Registry indexes them automatically.
Releases & Operations Release calendar and notes, the reconciliation register, incidents, runbook index. The registers are label-driven and populate themselves.
Teams One thin page per team: members, rituals, roadmap, and links to what the team owns. Product documentation does not live here.
Governance & Assurance The entry point for audit, risk and security. Controls matrix, governance roll-ups, security evidence. Mostly live views over content that lives elsewhere.
 
Adding a new page — the three rules
1
 Create it in the right zone, from the template.
Use the sidebar + on the intended parent page, and start from the standard template (ADR, release note, reconciliation, team page). New data products are copied from the master under _Master Copies — ask before creating one from scratch.
2
 Label it when you create it.
Every page gets its concern label (adr, recon, release, runbook…) plus the label of every product or component it touches. The same labels go on Jira tickets. Labels are how the registers, roll-ups and product pages stay current — an unlabelled page is invisible. The full list is on the Label & Metadata Taxonomy page; if you need a label that does not exist, add it there first.
3
 Link, don't copy.
If the content already exists somewhere else, link to it. Team pages link to products; product pages link to the paved road and record only their deviations; audit evidence is always a link, never a copy. Duplicated content goes stale and fails audits.
 
What changes in Jira
One thing: label your tickets at creation, using the same taxonomy as Confluence — the product or component the ticket relates to, e.g. cdp-surveillance or comp-orchstration. A correctly labelled ticket appears automatically on the relevant product page in Confluence. Boards, sprints and workflows are not changing.
Timeline
This sprint — structure built; one FDP documented end-to-end as the pilot. Next sprint — remaining products stamped out; old pages move across From then on — new content goes in the new structure only. The old space becomes read-only before being archived.
Nothing is being deleted — if you can't find something, it has moved, and the search and the A–Z index on the home page will get you there. Questions, or a label you think is missing: raise it on Teams and we'll sort it.
