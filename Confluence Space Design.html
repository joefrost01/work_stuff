Lab Knowledge Architecture · Confluence Redesign

One space, every audience

A Confluence structure that allows us to meet all needs — organised around data products, thin on teams, with audit, risk and security served by views rather than copies.

AudienceLab teams · Assurance partners DateJune 2026 StatusProposal · for discussion
The design problem

A tree can only have one shape

Teams want to navigate by team. Audit, risk and security want consistent paths to evidence. Architecture, infrastructure and process apply to everything. One hierarchy can't serve all three — so we don't ask it to.

Rule 01

Organise by product

The hierarchy follows the longest-lived thing: ODP → FDP → CDP. Teams reorganise; data products persist. A reorg costs the space nothing.

Rule 02

Teams stay thin

Team pages hold rituals, roadmaps and people — and link to everything they own. Teams never host product documentation.

Rule 03

Cross-cutting = views

Assurance, releases, reconciliations and Jira work surface through labels, not duplicate pages. Written once, seen everywhere relevant.

The principle in one line: write once, organised by product — surface many ways, via templates and labels.

Link up, never copy down: product pages reference the paved road and record only their deviations — which makes exceptions visible and searchable, a gift to audit.

Every product page — same eight children

  • Overview & ownership — PO, squad, status, criticality
  • Data model & contracts — schemas, dictionary (link to Collibra), versioning
  • Governance & controls — classification, retention, access
  • Lineage — link to Collibra

Predictability is the accommodation. When audit asks "show me governance for X", the answer is in the same place relative to any product — ODP, FDP or CDP.

Releases & Operations — the run-time zone

Change and run concerns get their own zone rather than being squeezed into products or process — because they're what operations, support and assurance check daily.

Releases

One page per release

  • Calendar & schedule, process & gates
  • Each release note labelled release + every product and component it touches
  • "What changed for product X" is a label query, not a curated page - based on Jira labels and release tag
Reconciliations

One page per test cycle

  • Framework lives in Standards; instances live here
  • Purpose, scope, owner, schedule, break process, evidence location
  • Register self-assembles by label: by product, component or frequency
Run

Incidents & support

  • Incident log & post-incident reviews
  • Support model & rotas
  • Runbook index — label-driven, runbooks themselves live with their products

Same pattern as everything else: a release or a rec is written once, tagged with the products it touches, and appears automatically on product pages, the register, and assurance roll-ups.

Zone detail · 4 of 4

Teams stay thin · Assurance gets a hub

👥 Teams

One page per team, mirroring the org slide: leadership, the five platform teams, the shared data product squad, and the two feature squads.

  • Mission, members, rituals, roadmap, Jira board link
  • Links to owned products and components — never hosted content
  • A reorg means moving links, not migrating documentation

🛡 Governance & Assurance

Audit, risk & controls and security never navigate the team structure — they enter here, and every page underneath is a live view.

  • Controls matrix: risk → control → evidence links into product pages
  • Governance, recon and control-test roll-ups by label
  • Security: access model summary, assessment evidence index
  • Audit workspaces — evidence by link, never by copy

Why this works: the hub holds almost no original content. If the underlying page moves or changes, the assurance view stays current — there is nothing to go stale.

Tagging & taxonomy

Labels assemble the virtual spaces

One taxonomy, defined once under Standards, used verbatim in Confluence and Jira:

FamilyPatternExamples

A ticket or page tagged cdp-surveillance + recon surfaces on the product page, the recon register, and assurance roll-ups — zero curation.

The "Surveillance CDP virtual space" — never built, always assembled:

Surveillance CDP cdp-surveillance
├─ Product page (the only real home)
├─ ADRs touching it          ← label query
├─ Releases that changed it   ← label query
├─ Its reconciliations        ← label query
├─ Its runbooks               ← label query
└─ Jira work in flight        ← JQL ↓
labels = cdp-surveillance
ORDER BY updated DESC

Embedded with the Jira Issues macro — the same query works for any product or component label.

Taxonomy & virtual spaces09 · 10
Making it stick

Three small habits

1 · Label at creation — labels are a field on Jira screens and pre-baked into every Confluence template, so authors never think about them.

2 · Templates are law — every data product, release, rec and ADR starts from the standard template. Predictable structure is what assurance buys.

3 · Every page has an owner — a named team from the org chart, reviewed quarterly. Ownership is what stops the operating layer rotting.
